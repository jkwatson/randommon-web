specialSphinx
  It poses a riddle about what belongs to you but is used more by others — a correct answer earns free passage and the name of the dungeon's master; wrong answers invoke a curse of misfortune lasting a day
  It asks what grows stronger the more you take from it — answer correctly and it names one secret of this level; answer wrong and it attacks, but breaks off at half HP and departs rather than die
  Its riddle has no correct answer — what it is actually testing is whether the party will admit they don't know rather than guess; honest uncertainty passes, confident wrongness fails
  It poses three riddles but only asks whichever it judges hardest for this particular party; it has been watching since the party entered the dungeon and will tailor the question accordingly
  It offers a bargain: answer a riddle, or pay in a year of life — it cannot enforce the latter and hopes the party won't call the bluff; it has grown bored of its post
  It guards something it no longer cares about and is hoping to be relieved of the obligation — the riddle is a formality and it will hint broadly if the party shows patience

// ── Hazard ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
dungeonHazard
  Bad air — torches gutter and heads swim; each turn of exposure, CON save DC 12 or gain disadvantage on all rolls; three consecutive failures = unconscious for 1 hour; fresh air revives immediately
  Unstable ceiling — cracks web the stone overhead; any running, shouting, or combat triggers a collapse check (DEX save DC 12, 2d6 damage on a fail); a catastrophic collapse blocks the passage and deals 4d6
  Rising water — the room is flooding; movement halved when waist-deep, swimming required when neck-deep (STR DC 12 each round or go under); exits submerge in 1d6+2 turns unless the source is stopped
  Toxic spore bloom — disturbing the fungal growth releases a choking cloud that lingers for 10 minutes; CON save DC 15 or take 1d6 poison damage and suffer disadvantage on attacks and saves for 1d4 hours
  Extreme cold — breath fogs, extremities numb; each 10 minutes of exposure, CON save DC 12 or take 1 CON damage; metal armour wearers save at disadvantage; any fire source negates the effect entirely
  Hollow floors — flagstones ring hollow underfoot; any point bearing more than one character's weight, DEX save DC 12 or fall through to the room below (2d6 damage, 1d6 with a soft landing below)
  Choke-damp — odourless and invisible, heavier than air; candles and torches gutter out 1 round before it turns lethal; CON save DC 15 each round or take 1d4 CON damage; CON 0 = death; 1 hour of rest in fresh air restores all lost CON
  Radiant heat — walls pulse warmth; each 10 minutes of exposure, CON save DC 12 or take 1d4 fire damage; metal armour wearers save at disadvantage and take an extra 1d4 on a failure
  Acid seep — slow drip from above, nearly invisible; leather, rope, and organic bindings degrade and fail after 10 minutes of exposure; a direct drip deals 1d4 acid damage per round; metal or stone held overhead provides cover
  Magnetic anomaly — metal is strongly attracted toward one wall; metal armour wearers make STR save DC 12 each round or be pulled 10 ft toward the wall; pinned at the wall, STR DC 15 to pull free; unarmoured characters are unaffected
  Flash flood channel — this passage fills in seconds when rain falls above; while weather is stormy, 1-in-6 chance of surge each turn; when it hits, DEX save DC 15 or take 2d8 bludgeoning damage and be swept to the lowest exit
  Absolute darkness — no torch, lantern, or candle functions here; magical light provides dim illumination at half range only; all attacks without darkvision are at disadvantage; spells requiring sight of a target cannot be cast

dungeonHazardDetail
  Consider what warning sign is visible before the hazard becomes dangerous.
  Consider how the hazard might be turned against enemies caught here.
  Consider that the hazard may have shaped or displaced whatever lives nearby.
  Consider whether the hazard can be mitigated, bypassed, or removed entirely.
  Consider placing the hazard at a chokepoint the party must pass through.
  Consider what happens if the hazard activates while the party is already in combat.

  A floor mosaic that, when viewed from the right angle, shows a rough map of this level — one marked chamber does not appear to exist anywhere in the dungeon; it does
