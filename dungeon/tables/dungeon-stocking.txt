dungeonHazard
  Bad air — torches gutter and heads swim; prolonged exposure causes nausea then unconsciousness
  Unstable ceiling — cracks web the stone overhead; loud noise or running risks collapse
  Rising water — the room is slowly flooding; exits may be submerged within the hour
  Toxic spore bloom — disturbing the thick fungal growth releases a choking cloud
  Extreme cold — breath fogs, extremities numb; metal armor burns to the touch
  Hollow floors — flagstones ring hollow underfoot; concentrated weight may give way
  Choke-damp — odorless, invisible, heavier than air; candles die first, then people
  Radiant heat — walls pulse warmth; prolonged exposure in metal armor becomes agonizing
  Acid seep — slow drip from above, nearly invisible; dissolves leather and rope over time
  Magnetic anomaly — metal is strongly attracted toward one wall; armor drags at its wearer
  Flash flood channel — this low passage fills entirely within seconds when rain falls above
  Absolute darkness — no natural light source functions here; even magical light dims and fades

  Consider whether the hazard can be mitigated, bypassed, or removed entirely.
  Consider placing the hazard at a chokepoint the party must pass through.
  Consider what happens if the hazard activates while the party is already in combat.
