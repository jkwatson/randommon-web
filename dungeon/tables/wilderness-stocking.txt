  Flash flood channel — the stream is rising fast from rain higher up
  Unstable slope — scree and loose stone; one wrong step starts a slide
  Dense thornbrake — passable, but slow, and the thorns cut exposed skin
  A wasp nest the size of a barrel, recently disturbed
  Quicksand — deceptively firm at the margin, treacherous in the middle
  Cliff edge — forty feet to broken rock, hidden by the vegetation
  Sinkhole field — the ground collapses in random spots without warning
  Wildfire smoke — thick enough to cut visibility to a few yards
  Ice-coated stone — a crossing that looks easy and has broken ankles before
  Rockslide beginning — pebbles skittering down announce what follows
  Flash freeze — temperature drops as an unnatural cold moves in fast

wildernessHazardDetail
  Consider what warning sign is visible before the hazard becomes dangerous.
  Consider whether the hazard can be bypassed with time, ingenuity, or local knowledge.
  Consider what happens if the party is caught mid-crossing when it activates.
  Consider how the hazard might affect a pursuer.
  Consider placing something worth recovering just past the worst of it.
  Consider whether locals know about this — and whether they'd warn travelers.
